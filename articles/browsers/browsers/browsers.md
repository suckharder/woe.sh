# Browsers
> ⚠️ Any time I mention privacy, take it with a grain of salt. Your online privacy depends on a multitude of factors, including your habits and operational security. None of these browsers will magically make you untraceable.

Browsers are a **fruitful** topic. After all, it is how most of us interact with the internet most of the time. That is precisely why I consider it a pretty ***important choice***. Below, you'll find a list of browsers *(along with a small amount of extra information)* that I either recommend out-right, or find intriguing in some way, shape or form.

> 📌 Note: This is a desktop-focused resource.

## Introduction

### 🐐 My catch-all recommendation

**Brave Browser.** Despite my reservations, and Brave's past controversies, it's still a ***really good*** browser - certainly *better* than what the *majority* of people use. Even using the out-of-the-box configuration will majorly improve your online ***privacy and security***, especially coming from something like *Chrome*.

---

### ❤️‍🔥 My picks

Much like with operating systems, I recommend a 3-way split. Not only are you hedging the "best of each", you're also compartmentalizing your online activity.

1. **Main "everyday browsing"**

**Mozilla Firefox + Phoenix config.** 90% of all browsing. Decent privacy and security, along with decent compatibility and usability. Jack of all trades, master of none. Well-rounded. Perfect for daily use.

2. **Secondary "generic addition"**

**Microsoft Edge.** *Yes, really*. Picking Edge specifically was *mostly arbitrary*, but it serves its purpose well. As a secondary browser, it proves helpful every time you **don't** necessarily care about privacy, or other features, but you REQUIRE full compatibility and usability. This can be anything from *work/school-related tasks, internet banking, or using a heavy/complex web app*.

3. **Tertiary "tinfoil hat mode"**

**Mullvad Browser.** Maximum privacy, maximum security. Enough said.

## Browser list

### Firefox

Indubitably, Mozilla's reputation took a hit [\[context\]](https://arstechnica.com/tech-policy/2025/02/firefox-deletes-promise-to-never-sell-personal-data-asks-users-not-to-panic "Ars Technica"). **However**, Firefox remains **open-source**, and even in its default configuration I believe it to be *streets ahead of Chrome*. What it does perfectly, is provide a ***strong foundation for being exactly the browser you want it to be*** - be it your main "all-around" browser, or a browser tailored to a specific use-case. There are multiple ready made configs, or you can roll your own.

*Generally speaking*, I recommend base Firefox over using a *fork*. I think the author of Phoenix puts it well: [\[read\]](https://codeberg.org/celenity/Phoenix/wiki/Why-Firefox "Phoenix Wiki").

Download **Firefox**: [Click \[official link\]](https://www.firefox.com "Mozilla")
